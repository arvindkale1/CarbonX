Our Focus Areas

Soil Carbon Credits

Soil carbon credits (SCCs) are tradable offsets earned by farmers who adopt practices that store more carbon (carbon sequestration) or reduce greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions from their soil, with one credit equaling one ton of CO2 equivalent; farmers sell these verified credits on voluntary markets to companies needing to offset their own emissions, creating new income while promoting healthier, more resilient agricultural systems

Water Carbon Credits

Water carbon credits are tradable certificates earned by projects that save or improve water, similar to carbon credits, by reducing emissions or enhancing natural processes (like wetlands) that store carbon, offering a way to fund water conservation/quality and helping companies offset their environmental footprint, linking water management to climate action

Industrial Carbon Credits

Industrial carbon credits are tradable permits, each representing one ton of CO2e reduced or removed, used by industries to meet emission limits in compliance markets or offset voluntary emissions, funding projects like renewables, energy efficiency, and carbon capture to lower overall pollution through 'cap-and-trade' systems or voluntary purchases
